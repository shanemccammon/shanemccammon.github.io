PCB Design and Manufacturing

Board 1: Astable 555 Timer

A PCB implementing an astable 555 timer. Designed to demonstrate timing circuits and layout considerations for simple oscillator circuits.

View Board 1 Report (PDF)

Board 2: Good vs Bad Layout Demonstration

A demonstration board using hex inverters to illustrate the effects of PCB layout on signal integrity and noise — compares good and bad layout practices.

View Board 2 Report (PDF)

Board 3: The "Golden Arduino"

A custom Arduino-compatible board designed to optimize noise performance and reduce EMI through careful component placement and grounding strategies. Uses ATMega328P bootloaded with Arduino Uno.

View Board 3 Report (PDF)

Board 4: Instrument Droid

A four-layer PCB designed to test and characterize voltage sources and Voltage Regulator Modules (VRMs) as an Arduino shield that can be attached to any Arduino Uno R3

Power Electronics

Follow-up lab course to Intro to Power Electronics: developed a power conditioning system (buck converter, cascaded boost converter, and inverter) for an 85W PV panel to charge a 12V deep cycle battery and provide 120VAC output. The converters operated at 100KHz and contained custom inductors and transformers that were hand-wrapped to specification. Please review lab reports for more specific design and measurement information as well as schematics.

Buck Converter

Buck converter to step down voltage and limit current and Maximum Power Point Tracking (MPPT) for battery charging. Efficiency: 86%

Cascaded Boost Converter

Cascaded boost converter to boost panel voltage to 150VDC to feed the inverter circuit. Efficiency: 81%
